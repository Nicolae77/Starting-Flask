What is the digital world?

Today's world is a digital world from simple online shopping to sophisticated industrial machines. As soon as you access the Internet, you are in direct contact with digital world. Everything is online. Customers search online for the products they need, go online to get more information about almost anything, and go online to chat with friends. Whenever a customer goes online, a company has a chance to connect with them creating a win-win relationship.

Importance of digital world in our community.

Even if you are in some other part of the world, digital technology allows you to connect with friends, family, and work remotely. You can communicate using words, audio, video, and other media. One of the best ways technology has changed our lives is in keeping us safer. Through technology, we're living longer than ever before. While there have been countless technology advances in hospitals and medicine, there are also new technologies we can use at home. Although it makes our lives easier, the digital world has some risks that we must take into account.

Definition and types of data.

Data is a value that represents something. The data is transmitted over network in bits, where the bits are created from zero and ones.

Data can be :

  • String or (str) – is a combination of any characters that appear on a keyboard, such as letters, numbers and symbols.
  • Character or (char) – is used for single letters.
  • Integer or (int) – used for whole number.
  • Float or (real) – represents decimal numbers.

On the other hand, information is data put into context.

Data has two types of measurements such as:

Storage
  • 1 byte = 8 bits
  • 1 kilobyte(kb) = 1024 bytes
  • 1 megabyte(mb) = 1024 kilobytes
  • 1 gigabyte(gb) = 1024megabyte
  • 1 terabyte (TB) = 1024 gigabytes
Speed
  • 1 Kbps(kilobyte per second)
  • 1 Mbps(Megabyte per second)
  • 1 Gbps(Gigabyte per second)s

Binary, Decimal and Hexadecimal Explanation.

A bit is a binary digit, which is the smallest unit of data measurement.

Bit = Binary Digit

OFF = 0 and ON = 1

Binary value = 0 and 1

Example of electrical cable either has current or doesn't.
No current = 0 Current = 1

Computers use powers of 2 because two is the base of the binary system.
